Parametric Nonlinear System Identification

Sloppiness, Information Gain, and Practical Strategies

Register Now

Abstract

This full-day workshop addresses fundamental and practical challenges in parametric nonlinear system identification, with a particular focus on sloppiness, identifiability, and information-theoretic experiment design. While many modern approaches emphasize predictive accuracy, they often overlook the structure of parameter uncertainty and its implications for interpretation, validation, and control.

The concept of sloppiness—where widely varying parameter combinations yield similar system outputs—poses significant challenges in understanding model behavior and ensuring robustness. This workshop introduces a geometric perspective on parameter spaces, enabling participants to distinguish between stiff and sloppy directions using sensitivity analysis and Fisher information.

Building on recent advances, the workshop integrates Bayesian inference techniques with a β-information gain framework to design experiments that are maximally informative. Participants will learn how these concepts can be applied to reduce posterior uncertainty, improve model reliability, and enhance decision-making in data-driven control systems.

Through a combination of theory, practical insights, and hands-on case studies, the workshop equips participants with tools to rethink nonlinear system identification from an information-centric perspective, ultimately bridging the gap between model accuracy, interpretability, and robust control design.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand sloppiness as a geometric property
  • Analyze sensitivity spectra
  • Apply β-information gain
  • Improve prediction and control strategies
